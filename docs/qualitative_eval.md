**Prompt:**
```
The capital of France is Paris. The capital of Germany is

**Continuation:**
```

- The capital of Switzerland is Frankfurt, while the rest of the former Soviet republics is called

- The capital of the former Soviet republics is Moscow, while the rest of the former Soviet

**Assessment:** Incorrect on the very first token — Germany's capital is Berlin, not Strasbourg. The model then pattern-matches to a list-like encyclopedic style and continues generating capitals of European countries, several of which are also wrong (Switzerland → Bern, not Frankfurt). This is a known failure mode of FineWeb-Edu-trained models: they produce plausible text structure around facts without reliable factual grounding.

## Summary Analysis

### What these outputs demonstrate

**Linguistic fluency is real.** Every sample except Prompt 5 (code) produces grammatically correct English with correct paragraph and sentence structure. The model has absorbed writing conventions from FineWeb-Edu and applies them consistently.

**Factual reliability is weak.** Of the 8 prompts, 0 produce a factually accurate continuation. The model confabulates with confidence — it produces plausible-sounding citations, capital cities, and scientific explanations that are wrong. This is expected for a pretrain-only model: FineWeb-Edu trains on text about facts, not on fact-retrieval as a task.

**Domain structure is partially learned.** The model recognizes that machine learning prompts belong to a technical-explainer genre, geography prompts to an encyclopedic list genre, and fairy tales to a mythological narrative genre. It defaults to the right register even when the specific content is wrong.

**Code is the weakest domain.** Prompt 5 collapsed entirely to whitespace, consistent with the earlier finding in `docs/eval_results.md` (Test 5) that code is the most repetition-prone domain and the vocabulary for code continuations is extremely narrow at moderate temperatures.

**The EOS-then-new-document pattern appears.** Prompt 8 illustrates a known behavior: the model emits `<|endoftext|>` partway through generation and continues with a new document from its training distribution. This is undesirable for interactive use but is normal for pretrain-only models and would be suppressed by sampling with an EOS stop condition.

### Expected improvement path

Fine-tuning on instruction-following data (even a small SFT pass) would dramatically improve factual precision and eliminate the topic-drift and EOS-in-middle patterns. The underlying language model quality (fluency, domain awareness) provides a solid foundation for fine-tuning.
